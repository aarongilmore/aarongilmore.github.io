Introduction

With Mobile World Congress on the horizone we were put to the task of brewing a series of beers to take with us and share with the attendees. This pale ale was the first of three beers we brewed and it's packed with flavour.

The Mash

The malt profile of this beer should form a nice background for the hops to shine. Regular pale ale malt makes up most of the bill. The combination of two different crystal malts will make the colour a little more interesting while rounding off the malt flavour profile. The Munich malt will add just a little complexity. The mash went really well. The temperature was maintained at 66oC for 60 minutes, ensuring a good conversion of the starches into fermentable sugars.

Hopping

This beer was all about the hops. I was excited to try to get as much flavour out of the hop additions without unbalancing the final beer. Warrior early in the boil gave the beer a floral, citrusy finish. I was curious to see what the Nelson Sauvin hop would add through multiple hop additions. It contributed a wonderfully floral piney note, similar to a Sauvignon Blanc. The Cascade and Centennial, combined with the Nelson Sauvin throughout the rest of the boil, and in the dry hop, rounded the aroma and flavour off with a big burst of fresh citrus.

Fermentation

The fermentation went well. Nice and clean. The US-05 yeast ferments clean, allowing all of Brewbot’s hard work to shine through. Fermentation is where most of the mistakes can happen in a beer, so I was delighted that it went according to plan. After fermentation was complete, I crash cooled it to clear the beer and then kegged and bottled it.
