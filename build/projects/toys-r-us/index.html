Digitally Transforming Toys"R"Us

Crippled by leveraged debt, Toys“R”Us turned to its most profitable store for a lifeline: e-commerce. During a two year span we helped digitaly tranform the UK e-commerce into a profitable earner that was easy for customers to use. Unfortunately, it wasn't enough to save them from their much larger financial woes ↗.

Role(s) UX research & design, project management, prototyping, front-end development
Period 2017–2018
Link Offine

Introduction

I teamed up with the folks at Dawson Andrews when I freelancing to help them deliver a new experience for Toys"R"Us. In this case study I will showcase some of the issues at the heart of the problem of the e-commerce organisation, the approach to the solution, and the highest performing design solutions we provided and implemented.

But first, some key highlights from this project:

  • Overall Conversion Rate increased by 20.74%
  • Cart Abandonment on Mobile decreased by 74.6%
  • Mobile Revenues increased by 42.3%
  • Cart to Checkout Conversion was up 210.4%
  • Add to Cart went up by x3

On Smaller Screens

For the best part of a year I worked on various aspects of the Toy"R"Us redesign but the area I really focused on was mobile. With 60%+ users experiencing the website on mobile we knew it would be a key area to target in our digital transformation.

Through research and development we were able to produce a simplified mobile experiece that saw some great improvments in conversion:

  • Mobile Conversion Rate increased 46.33%
  • Tablet Conversion Rate increased 6.3%
  • Mobile Penetration (i.e. what percentage on mobile of overall revenues) increased 14.1%
