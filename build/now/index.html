Now

I'm hunkering down in Belfast and longing to see my friends and family. Things have been up and down with more frequency than ever; today—May 11—is a good day. Meditation, writing, and reading is helping me through this difficult time.

Fortunately, I'm still employed and enjoying work for the most part. Sometimes though, with the current state of the world, the work feels insignificant. I'm doing a lot of work on the digital side of GAA, a sports organisation here in Ireland. On the side, I'm writing more than ever about design and trying to distill some thoughts into something readable.

I do have a good amount of free time in the evenings and at the weekend. This time is spent reading: Nan Shepard's The Living Mountain; Lucy Ellmann's Ducks, Newburyport; Lyra McKee's Lost, Found, Remembered. Cooking a lot, too. From caesar salads to fried chicken sandwiches and everything in between. I was baking before it was cool.
